## üéØ NAMING CRITERIA

### Must-Haves
- Available .com or .io domain (or reasonable variant)
- No direct fintech/payments conflicts
- Works in English and Spanish markets
- Easy to pronounce and spell
- Trademark-able

### Nice-to-Haves
- Evokes: smoothness, trust, crossing, infrastructure, settlement
- Short (1-2 syllables preferred)
- Memorable
- Works as a verb ("Just [name] it")

### Brand Values to Evoke
- Removing complexity
- Smooth/frictionless settlement  
- Trust/governance for enterprise
- Crossing boundaries (protocols ‚Üî local rails)
- Infrastructure that "just works"
- The "ground station" connecting stablecoins to local money

## üìä KEY INSIGHTS

### Why Naming is So Hard in Fintech
- Every simple English word is taken by a funded startup
- The "simple word + payments vertical" formula is exhausted
- Even invented names often have conflicts
- LATAM-specific terms sometimes have existing players
- Fruit names are surprisingly crowded (Mango, Guava, Kiwi all blocked)
- Person names that suggest finance roles also crowded
- "Union" synonyms (Alliance, Accord, Bond, Guild) all have major conflicts
- Portuguese/Italian words have hidden landmines (Elo = Brazil's national card network!)
- "Zen" prefix is extremely crowded in fintech
- Astronomy names (Pollux) may have LATAM conflicts

### What's Working
- Spanish words less saturated than English
- Invented combinations with Spanish roots
- Names that don't obviously scream "payments" may be clearer

### Categories Exhausted (High Conflict Rate)
- Simple English payment words
- Fruit names
- Agent/role names (Herald, Envoy, etc.)
- "Flow/smooth/clear" metaphors
- Union synonyms (Alliance, Accord, Bond, Guild)
- Textile weaving names (Twill)
- Swahili words (Rahisi)

### Categories Worth Exploring
- Obscure Spanish words
- Completely invented 2-syllable names
- Non-obvious metaphors (astronomy, geology, architecture)
- Portuguese/Italian words
- Weather/wind names
- Tool/instrument names
