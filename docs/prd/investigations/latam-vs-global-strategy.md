# LATAM vs Global Strategy Analysis

**Last Updated:** January 2026  
**Source:** Competitive analysis, market research, strategic planning

---

## Executive Summary

PayOS faces a strategic choice: stay LATAM-focused or expand globally. This analysis concludes that **staying LATAM-focused is the higher-probability path** to success. Going global prematurely risks becoming a worse-funded Crossmint clone. However, if going global, the B2B procurement vertical is the most interesting white space.

---

## Without LATAM: What Differentiators Remain?

| Differentiator | With LATAM Focus | Without LATAM Focus |
|----------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Pix/SPEI/Nequi settlement** | **Unique** — no one else has it | Irrelevant |
| **Circle CPN integration** | **Advantage** — zero float | Same as anyone on Circle |
| **Protocol-agnostic (x402/AP2/ACP)** | Strong | Same as Crossmint |
| **White-label B2B model** | Strong | Same as Gr4vy |
| **AI-native dashboard** | Differentiator | Nice-to-have, not moat |
| **Regulatory/corridor expertise** | **Deep** in LATAM | Would need to build everywhere |

**The honest answer:** Without LATAM, you're competing head-to-head with Crossmint on protocol support and Gr4vy on orchestration — both better funded and further along.

## Global Strategy Options

### Option A: Out-Execute on Multi-Protocol

**Strategy:** Become the **true** protocol-agnostic settlement layer that works with ALL protocols, including Stripe's ACP. Be the Switzerland of agentic payments.

**Requirements:**
- Stripe partnership or deep ACP integration
- Visa Trusted Agent Protocol support
- Mastercard Agent Pay support
- x402 facilitator status
- AP2 credentials provider

**Timeline:** 12-18 months to build comprehensive coverage

**Capital Required:** $10M+ (competing with $23M Crossmint)

**Risk:** They can copy this faster than you can build it

**Win Probability:** Low

---

### Option B: Win on Partner Model (Anti-Crossmint)

**Strategy:** Position as the **partner-first** infrastructure that never competes with customers.

**Crossmint's Tension:** When MoneyGram uses Crossmint, MoneyGram's customers become Crossmint's customers too. Crossmint owns the wallet.

**Pitch:** *"Crossmint wants to be the wallet. We want to power yours."*

**Requirements:**
- Contractual commitment to never go direct-to-consumer
- True white-label (no PayOS branding anywhere)
- Partner success team, not just developer docs
- Revenue share models that align incentives

**Timeline:** 12-18 months

**Capital Required:** $5-10M

**Risk:** This is a positioning/trust play, not a technical moat. Crossmint could make the same promise.

**Win Probability:** Medium

---

### Option C: Vertical Focus — B2B Procurement

**Strategy:** Instead of geographic focus, pick **B2B Procurement** as a vertical where no one is building.

**The Gap:** No one building specifically for: *"An AI agent that handles purchasing for a company — finds vendors, negotiates, pays, tracks delivery."*

**Requirements:**
- Procurement-specific features (PO matching, approval workflows, vendor management)
- ERP integrations (NetSuite, SAP, QuickBooks)
- Multi-currency treasury management
- AP automation capabilities
- Escrow for milestone payments

**Timeline:** 12-18 months

**Capital Required:** $5-10M

**Risks:**
- Enterprise sales cycle is long
- Requires deep integrations with legacy systems
- Competing with AP automation players (Bill.com, Tipalti, Coupa)

**Win Probability:** Medium — genuinely underserved

**Validation:** Skyfire CEO noted "B2B payments are probably the most predictable."

---

### Option D: Settlement Layer for Orchestrators

**Strategy:** Don't compete with Gr4vy — **power Gr4vy**. Partner with orchestration platforms.

**Pitch:** *"You orchestrate the protocols. We settle the money."*

**Targets:**
- Gr4vy (orchestration + agentic)
- Spreedly (multi-PSP)
- Primer (orchestration)
- Corefy (orchestration)

**Requirements:**
- Partnerships with orchestration platforms
- Settlement-as-a-Service API
- Multi-chain, multi-stablecoin support
- Treasury/liquidity management

**Timeline:** 12 months

**Capital Required:** $5M+

**Risk:** You become a commodity layer with little pricing power

**Win Probability:** Low — low margin, commoditized

## The Brutal Math

| Factor | Go Global | Stay LATAM |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| **TAM** | Massive ($190T cross-border) | Large focused (~$150B remittance corridors) |
| **Competition** | Crossmint ($23M), Skyfire ($10M), Gr4vy ($30M+), plus Stripe, Visa, Mastercard | Almost no one with depth |

| **Capital Required** | $10M+ to compete | $2-5M to dominate |

| **Win Probability** | Lower — better-funded competitors | Higher — geographic moat |

## Recommended Approach: LATAM First, Then Expand

### Phase 1: Own LATAM (Now - 12 months)
- Dominate Brazil/Mexico corridors with Pix/SPEI
- Get 3-5 regional fintech partners live
- Build regulatory expertise as moat
- Establish Circle CPN integration advantage

### Phase 2: Add Adjacent Corridors (12-24 months)
- US→LATAM remittances (massive volume)
- LATAM→Europe (growing diaspora)
- Leverage LATAM partners expanding globally

### Phase 3: Selective Global (24+ months)
- Follow partners as they go global
- Add settlement rails in markets where you have demand
- Consider acquisition of regional players (Africa, SEA)

**The Insight:** Crossmint and Skyfire are trying to boil the ocean. You can own a region, prove the model, then expand from strength.

## Why LATAM Still Wins

### Market Readiness
- 61% of Brazilian shoppers use mobile for purchases
- Digital payment methods: 48% of eCommerce in 2024, projected 66% by 2030
- Pix processed $1.7T+ in 2024
- SPEI enables real-time settlement

### Competitive Timing
- Mastercard Agent Pay launching LATAM/Caribbean 2026
- 12+ month window before mainstream agentic commerce
- No direct competitors with Pix/SPEI depth

### Infrastructure Advantage
- Circle CPN mainnet live
- Alfred Pay validating Pix/SPEI off-ramps
- Rezolve Ai processed $1B+ stablecoin transactions in Brazil

### Regulatory Path (from PRD)
| Phase | Approach | Timeline | Cost |
|-------|----------|----------|------|
| Phase 1 (Now) | Pure SaaS — partners bring Circle accounts | Immediate | $0 |
| Phase 2 (Post-PMF) | API agent model — registration as tech provider | 3-6 months | ~$50K |
| Phase 3 (Scale) | Own licenses in key markets | 18-24 months | $2-7M Brazil, ~$500K Mexico |

## The One Exception: B2B Procurement

If going global without geographic focus, **B2B procurement agent vertical** is genuinely underserved:

- Skyfire sees it but isn't building for it specifically
- Crossmint is consumer/fintech focused
- Gr4vy is merchant checkout focused
- €31M+ raised in procurement AI — none do payment

**What This Would Look Like:**
- Integration with ERP systems (NetSuite, SAP)
- Multi-level approval workflows for agent-initiated purchases
- Vendor verification and management
- Invoice matching and reconciliation
- Stablecoin treasury management for global payables
- Escrow for milestone-based contracts

## Decision Framework

**Stay LATAM if:**
- Capital is limited ($2-5M available)
- Speed to revenue is priority
- Want defensible position within 12 months
- Circle CPN/Pix/SPEI is genuine advantage

**Go Global if:**
- Have access to $10M+ capital
- Willing to compete directly with Crossmint
- Have enterprise sales team ready
- Can build protocol partnerships quickly

**Go B2B Procurement if:**
- Want differentiation without geographic focus
- Willing to build ERP integrations
- Can handle enterprise sales cycles
- See procurement AI (Monq, Mercanis) as partners

---

## Conclusion

> **Going global prematurely is how you become a worse-funded Crossmint clone.**

PayOS's Circle Pix/SPEI integration is a 12-month head start. That advantage disappears globally. Stay LATAM-focused for now, dominate the region, then expand from strength.

The procurement AI opportunity (Option C) is valid but requires different capabilities and GTM motion. Could be pursued in parallel with LATAM geographic focus.
